About Mother's Day

Mother's Day is a special occasion celebrated around the world to honor and appreciate the love, care, and sacrifices of mothers and maternal figures. In many countries, including the United States, Canada, and Australia, it is observed on the second Sunday in May. The holiday was inspired by Anna Jarvis in the early 1900s as a way to honor her own mother, and it became an official U.S. holiday in 1914. People celebrate by giving flowers (especially carnations), cards, gifts, or spending quality time with their mothers. Traditions vary by countryтАФfor example, Mexico celebrates on May 10 with music and family gatherings, Thailand honors mothers on August 12, the birthday of Queen Sirikit, and the UK observes Mothering Sunday on the fourth Sunday of Lent. While customs differ, the heart of Mother's Day remains the same: showing gratitude and love to the women who nurture and guide us.
